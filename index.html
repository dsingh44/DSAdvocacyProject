What is climate change ?

Climate change refers to long-term changes in the Earth's average weather patterns, such as temperature, precipitation, and wind. It is largely caused by human activities, particularly the emission of greenhouse gases from burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and other industrial processes. Climate change is a problem because it leads to a range of negative impacts on ecosystems, human health, and the global economy, including more frequent and severe weather events, sea level rise, and food and water scarcity.
